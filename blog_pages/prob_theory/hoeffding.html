The Hoeffding Inequality

Suppose $S_n=\sum_{i=1}^{n}X_i$ is the sum of independent r.v.s $X_1, X_2, \cdots, X_n$, $X_i \in [a_i, b_i]$, then $\forall t > 0$, we have the following inequality: $$ \begin{aligned} P(S_n - E_{S_n} \ge t) &\le \operatorname{exp}\left(\frac{-2t^2}{\sum_{i=1}^{n}(b_i-a_i)^2}\right) \\ P(E_{S_n} - S_n \ge t) &\le \operatorname{exp}\left(\frac{-2t^2}{\sum_{i=1}^{n}(b_i-a_i)^2}\right) \end{aligned} $$
Application
  • Generation Error Bound
    By using the hoeffding inequality, we can find the generation error bound for finite hypothesis space $\mathcal{F}=\{f_1, f_2, \cdots, f_d\}$: $$ \forall f \in \mathcal{F}, \quad P\left(R(f) < \hat{R}(f) + \epsilon\right) \ge 1 - d \operatorname{exp}(-2N\epsilon^2) $$ where $R(f)=E[\mathcal{L}(\mathbf{Y}, f(\mathbf{X}))]$, $\hat{R}(f)=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\mathcal{L}(y_i, f(x_i))$.
    Hence, at least with the probability of $1-\delta$, we have $$ R(f) \le \hat{R}(f)+\epsilon(d, N, \delta), \quad \epsilon(d, N, \delta)=\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}\left(\log d +\log \frac{1}{\delta}\right)}$$
